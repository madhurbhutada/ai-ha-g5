  1. To speed up the drug discovery process

Graphical Neural Networks are used to establish a relationship between Target Protein and Disease.

How does GNN work?

Imagine a GNN as a system that understands relationships and connections between different pieces of data, represented as a graph. Each node in the graph corresponds to some entity, like a user or an item in a recommendation system, and the edges represent the relationships between them.
Now, when we say "understand," what we really mean is that the GNN can learn patterns from these connections. It does this by passing messages between nodes, updating their internal representations based on the information received from their neighbors. This process happens iteratively, allowing the network to capture increasingly complex relationships and dependencies within the data.
In simpler terms, the GNN looks at how things are connected to each other and learns from those connections to make better predictions or classifications.

Use of GNN in Drug Discovery

Step 1: Small molecule drugs can also be depicted as graphs, with atoms as nodes and chemical bonds as edges.
Step 2: Node feature vectors (X) are constructed by combining various properties of atoms, such as mass (m), electron number (e), and charge (c), denoted as: X=[m,e,c]
Step 3: These feature vectors are then stacked into a matrix X. To capture the underlying structure of molecules, the stacked feature matrix (X) is multiplied by the adjacency matrix (A), yielding: X’ = X x A, where X′ represents the transformed feature matrix capturing the structural relationships within molecules.


How does Variational Autoencoder (VAE) work?

It is made up of two parts-

  • Encoder
  • Decoder

The encoder network takes an input data point, such as an image, and maps it to a probability distribution in a lower-dimensional latent space.
During training, the VAE reconstructs input data points from sampled latent variables using a decoder network.
It is trained to minimize a reconstruction loss, ensuring accurate reconstruction, while also regularizing the latent space distribution to match a predefined prior distribution, typically a standard Gaussian.

Use of VAE in Drug Discovery

Step 1: The neural network converts the discrete input molecule into a Gaussian distribution.
Step 2: The latent variables are reparametrized against the mean and variance.
Step 3: The decoder generates a new molecule from the sampled latent space.
